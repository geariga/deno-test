H₂O - Water

Water, represented by the chemical formula H₂O, is a vital substance for all forms of life on Earth. It consists of two hydrogen atoms (H) bonded to one oxygen atom (O), forming a molecule.

Chemical Structure of Water

The structure of a water molecule consists of a bent shape, with the angle between the two hydrogen atoms being approximately 104.5 degrees. This gives water unique properties such as surface tension and its ability to dissolve many substances.

Properties of Water

  • Water is a universal solvent, meaning it can dissolve many substances.
  • It has a high specific heat, which helps regulate temperature.
  • Water expands when it freezes, which is why ice floats on water.
  • It is essential for many biological processes, such as respiration and photosynthesis.

Water covers about 71% of the Earth's surface, mostly in the form of oceans, lakes, and rivers. It is also present in the atmosphere as vapor and in glaciers as ice.
